<p><p>The below-knee prosthesis market features two primary types: exoskeleton and endoskeletal prostheses. Exoskeleton prostheses consist of an external framework that supports the residual limb, providing stability and strength, often allowing for greater mobility. In contrast, endoskeletal prostheses have a central support structure with an outer cosmetic cover, offering a lightweight and adjustable solution. Both types cater to varying user preferences and functional needs, aiming to enhance mobility and quality of life for amputees.</p></p>
