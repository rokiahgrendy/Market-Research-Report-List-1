<p><p>The galvanized steel plates market comprises two main types: hot-rolled and cold-rolled. Hot-rolled galvanized steel is produced by rolling the steel at elevated temperatures, resulting in thicker plates with better ductility and strength, commonly used in construction and heavy machinery. In contrast, cold-rolled galvanized steel is processed at room temperature, yielding thinner, smoother plates with superior surface finishes. Cold-rolled products are often preferred for automotive and appliance applications due to their tighter tolerances and enhanced aesthetics.</p></p>
