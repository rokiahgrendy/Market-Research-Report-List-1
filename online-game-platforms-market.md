<p><p>Online game platforms can be categorized into two primary market types: cloud-based and on-premise. Cloud-based platforms enable users to access games via the internet without the need for extensive hardware, offering flexibility and convenience. These platforms often rely on subscription models and provide instant updates. In contrast, on-premise markets require users to install games directly on their devices, ensuring offline accessibility. This model typically involves one-time purchases and may offer enhanced performance and control over the gaming experience.</p></p>
